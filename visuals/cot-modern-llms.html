Chain of Thought in Modern LLMs

How GPT, Claude, and Gemini leverage step-by-step reasoning

The CoT Process: Step-by-Step

1
User Input
You ask a complex question. The prompt can explicitly request step-by-step thinking with phrases like "Let's think step by step" or "Show your work."
"Sarah has twice as many apples as Tom. Tom has 5 apples. How many apples do they have together? Let's think step by step."
2
Internal Reasoning (Scratchpad)
The model breaks down the problem into intermediate steps. This "scratchpad" is generated token-by-token, allowing the model to catch errors as it goes.
Step 1: Tom has 5 apples.
Step 2: Sarah has twice as many, so Sarah has 5 Ã— 2 = 10 apples.
Step 3: Together they have 5 + 10 = 15 apples.
3
Final Answer
After completing the reasoning, the model generates the final, clean answer. The user sees both the scratchpad AND the answer, or sometimes just the answer depending on the tool.
Answer: Sarah and Tom have 15 apples together.
Why This Works: By explicitly generating intermediate reasoning, the model is more likely to arrive at the correct answer. It's not just pattern-matching; it's actually working through the logic. This is especially powerful for math, logic puzzles, and multi-step problems.

How Modern Chat Tools Use CoT

ChatGPT (GPT-4o)
Approach
Supports CoT implicitly and explicitly. Users can request step-by-step thinking.
Visibility
Shows reasoning in the chat, allowing users to see and critique each step.
Best For
Math problems, debugging, explaining complex concepts.
Example Prompt
"Solve this step by step: If X = 10..."
Claude (Anthropic)
Approach
Naturally inclined to reason through problems. No special prompt needed; it's built into training.
Visibility
Shows detailed reasoning by default, making the thinking process transparent.
Best For
Complex analysis, writing, research, and problems requiring careful thought.
Example Prompt
"Analyze this situation carefully..."
Gemini (Google)
Approach
Integrates CoT as part of its reasoning capabilities. Also supports "thinking" tokens for internal reasoning.
Visibility
Some reasoning is visible, though more advanced reasoning happens internally.
Best For
Information retrieval, search-based questions, and multi-modal reasoning.
Example Prompt
"Think about this carefully before answering..."

Practical Tips for Using CoT

Be Explicit When You Need It

For complex tasks, explicitly ask for step-by-step reasoning: "Let's think step by step" or "Show your working." Most modern LLMs will respond by generating intermediate reasoning.

Verify Intermediate Steps

When you see the reasoning, check each step for correctness. Even LLMs can make mistakes in their logic, but seeing the reasoning allows you to catch and correct them.

Use for Knowledge Work

CoT is especially effective for tasks requiring logic, math, debugging, and complex decision-making. It's less necessary for creative or open-ended tasks.

Combine with Other Techniques

CoT pairs well with other prompting techniques like few-shot learning (providing examples) or specifying output format. You can ask: "Solve this step by step, then format the answer as JSON."
