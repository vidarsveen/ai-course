Each layer represents a more specialized subset

Artificial Intelligence (AI)

The all-encompassing field for creating intelligent machines. Includes everything from basic rule-based systems and robot programming to advanced reasoning and problem-solving.

Machine Learning (ML)

A subset of AI where systems learn patterns from data rather than following explicit rules. Used in fraud detection, recommendation systems, and spam filters.

Deep Learning (DL)

A specialized branch of ML using multi-layered neural networks inspired by the human brain. Excels at complex tasks like image recognition, speech recognition, and natural language understanding.

Generative AI

AI systems that create new content rather than just analyzing existing data. Examples include DALL-E (images), Midjourney (art), and text generation models.

Large Language Models (LLMs)

A specialized form of Generative AI focused on language. Trained on massive text datasets to understand and generate human-like text. Examples: GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, Llama.

GPT / ChatGPT

Specific implementations of LLMs. GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer) is the architecture, and ChatGPT is the conversational product built on it.
